Common misconception:

  • statements (actually rumours) that are shaped into stories are believable. This is because people often naturally think in terms of narratives. But rumour itself is non-existent and highly malleable, making it easier and better suited to being narrated than the real thing.
  • spreading rumours can bring a sense of presence to oneself. This sense of existence is not a positive one, and the rumours that are spread may cause harm to others.
