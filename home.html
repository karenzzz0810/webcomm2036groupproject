Rumors

is defined as social psychology uses more elegantly the Latin word rumor (rumour in British English), meaning sound, voice or gossip. In social, economic, political, cultural and scientific communication, rumours refer to news that is presumed to be true and that is spread without confirmation or obviousness.


Fake news

is defined as those news stories that are false: the story itself is fabricated, with no verifiable facts, sources or quotes. These stories may be intentionally designed to mislead the reader or increase the click-through rate for economic incentives. Some fake news may include a few part of truth, but lack any contextualizing details while some stories may include basic confirmable facts, but are written in deliberately inflammatory language, leaves out relevant details or only presents one viewpoint.


Misinformation

is defined as “false information that is spread, regardless of intent to mislead. Misinformation is first recorded in the late 1500s, and combines information with the prefix mis-, meaning “wrong” or “mistaken.” Mistake, misspelling, and misunderstanding are some examples of other common words besides mis- that use misinformation to mean “wrong.”


Disinformation

is defined as false information deliberately and often covertly spread (as by the planting of rumors) in order to influence public opinion or obscure the truth Disinformation means “false information, as about a country’s military strength or plans, disseminated by a government or intelligence agency in a hostile act of tactical political subversion.” It is also used more generally to mean “deliberately misleading or biased information; manipulated narrative or facts; propaganda.” So, disinformation is misinformation that is knowingly (intentionally) spread.
